Process

How we
design

Our approach

We do not begin with a form. We begin with questions — about place, about people, about purpose. The architecture emerges from the rigour with which those questions are answered.

Every project at Axiom moves through a defined sequence of phases, each with its own depth and integrity. We resist the temptation to rush toward a solution before we fully understand the problem.

This process has been refined across 60 years and 400 projects — on every continent, at every scale.

The phases
01
Listen & Research
Understanding before answering
Every engagement begins with a sustained period of listening. We immerse ourselves in the client's world — understanding their operations, culture, aspirations and constraints. We survey the site extensively: its microclimate, its solar geometry, its acoustic landscape, its connection to the wider city or landscape. We study the precedents, the failures, the things that have been tried before. Only when we are genuinely saturated with understanding do we begin to draw.
02
Concept
The essential gesture
The concept phase is about finding the one organising idea that can unify every subsequent decision. We test many directions, but we commit to one — developing it with enough depth to withstand scrutiny. A strong concept is not a style or a formal signature; it is a strategic response to the conditions of the project. It answers the question: why should this building look and work the way it does?
03
Design Development
From idea to architecture
This is where the concept is tested against reality. We work across scales simultaneously — from the master plan to the door handle — because the quality of architecture depends on the coherence of decisions at every level. We integrate structural, environmental and mechanical engineering from the outset, not as a separate layer but as a co-author of form. Models are built, options are compared, and every decision is justified against the project's governing principles.
04
Technical Design
Precision at every detail
Technical design is not a reduction of ambition — it is its proof. Every joint, every junction, every material interface is resolved with the same care as the overall form. We produce complete, coordinated drawing packages that leave nothing ambiguous. Our technical team works hand-in-glove with specialist contractors, manufacturers and material researchers to ensure that what is drawn can be built — and that what is built matches the intent exactly.
05
Construction
Present on site, always
Architecture is made on site. We maintain full design authority through construction, with a dedicated team present at every stage. Decisions made in the field — the response to unexpected ground conditions, the resolution of a material substitution, the response to a changed programme — are made with the same quality of thought as those made in the studio. We do not disappear after planning approval.
06
Post-Occupancy
Buildings are not finished — they are inhabited
We return to every completed project at six months and two years after occupation. We measure performance against predictions, we speak with the people who live and work in the building, and we carry what we learn forward into future work. This is not a contractual obligation — it is a professional and ethical one. We are responsible for what we build, long after the keys are handed over.
Guiding principles
— 01
Integration over addition
Structure, environment, programme and form must be conceived together from the outset. A building that treats engineering as a constraint to be managed will never be as good as one where structure and architecture are inseparable.
— 02
Place as the brief
The site is not a blank canvas. It carries climate, history, culture and urban grain. Architecture that ignores these forces will always feel foreign. Architecture that responds to them will always feel inevitable.
— 03
Restraint as rigour
We resist the accumulation of features, materials and gestures. Our work is defined by the precision of what is left out as much as by what is included. Complexity of thought should produce simplicity of form.
— 04
Longevity by design
The most sustainable building is the one that is never demolished. We design for durability, adaptability and enduring relevance — so that the buildings we make are as useful in a hundred years as they are on opening day.
